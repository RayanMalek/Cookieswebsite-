04 Voice & Tone
01 Artisan

Every product is crafted by hand. The language reflects skill, intention, and care — never mass-produced.

02 Intimate

Cookiez'Annou is a personal brand. It speaks warmly, like a recommendation from someone you trust completely.

03 Refined

Premium without pretension. The copy is confident, precise, and never over-explains or oversells.

04 Sensory

Descriptions should evoke smell, texture, and warmth — not just list ingredients. Make them feel it before they taste it.

05 Usage — Do & Don't
Do
  • Use Cormorant Garamond for all large-format display text and product names.
  • Pair dark backgrounds with cream or gold typography only.
  • Let white space breathe — generous margins signal premium positioning.
  • Use the wave motif as a structural divider between sections.
  • Display prices in Cormorant Garamond with the "dt" superscript convention.
  • Keep photography warm-toned, close-up, and slightly dimmed at the edges.
  • Write product names in their original mixed-language form (e.g. Tiramicoo, Flankie).
Don't
  • Never use the logo on busy photographic backgrounds without a darkening overlay.
  • Never use more than two typefaces in a single layout.
  • Avoid bright, saturated colours outside the defined palette.
  • Do not use all-caps for body copy — only for labels and eyebrows.
  • Never stretch, rotate, or apply drop shadows to the wordmark.
  • Avoid generic stock imagery — always use real, in-house product photography.
  • Do not add decorative elements (stars, sparkles) — the wave and dot motifs are sufficient.
